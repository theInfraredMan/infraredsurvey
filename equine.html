What We Detect

What Can Infrared
Reveal in Horses?

Thermal cameras detect temperature differences as small as 0.1°C — making subclinical issues visible long before clinical signs appear.

🦵

Lameness & Inflammation

Heat signatures pinpoint the exact location of inflammation in limbs, joints, and hooves — guiding targeted treatment before lameness progresses.

🎯

Ligament & Tendon Stress

Stressed tendons and ligaments generate heat before they rupture. Infrared scanning is used as an early-warning tool by performance horse trainers.

🐎

Saddle Fit Analysis

A poorly fitting saddle creates pressure points that show as hot or cold spots on a post-ride thermal scan — protecting your horse's back and performance.

💪

Muscle Asymmetry

Comparing left-right thermal patterns reveals muscle imbalances and compensation patterns that indicate underlying pain or training issues.

🩺

Pre-Purchase Evaluation

Add an infrared scan to your pre-purchase exam to uncover hidden issues that may not yet show on X-ray or cause visible symptoms.

📈

Treatment Monitoring

Track healing progress over time with sequential thermal imaging — objectively confirming whether therapies are working.
