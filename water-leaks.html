The Method

Thermal Imaging Sees What
Your Eyes Can't

Water absorbs and releases heat differently than dry building materials. A FLIR thermal camera reveals those temperature differences — exposing exactly where moisture is hiding, even behind finished surfaces.

🌡️

Thermal Differential

Wet materials stay cooler as moisture evaporates. The camera maps those cool zones precisely — whether behind drywall, under tile, or in a concrete slab.

📷

Non-Destructive

No cutting, no drilling, no exploratory demolition. We scan the structure and deliver a report showing exactly where to open — and nowhere else.

🔍

Source Pinpointing

We follow the moisture trail back to the origin — not just where damage appears. Knowing the source saves costly repeat repairs.

📄

Documented Report

You receive a full written report with thermal images, location diagrams, severity assessment, and recommended corrective action — delivered within 24 hours.

Where We Look

Every Type of Water Leak.
One Inspection.

Infrared thermal imaging works across all building types and leak scenarios — commercial, residential, and industrial.

🏠

Slab & Foundation Leaks

Hot water line breaks under concrete show as warm anomalies on the slab surface — located precisely before any concrete is cut.

🏠

Roof & Ceiling Leaks

Moisture trapped in roofing insulation or above ceiling tiles maps clearly after rain or HVAC condensation events.

🛌

Plumbing Behind Walls

Slow pipe leaks inside finished walls — the type that cause hidden mold — are revealed by the moisture gradient before structural damage sets in.

🏛️

HVAC Condensate Leaks

Clogged drain pans and condensate line failures soak insulation and decking. Thermal imaging finds the full extent of saturation, not just the drip point.

🏗️

Building Envelope Intrusion

Water infiltrating through windows, doors, expansion joints, or flashing failures — mapped across the full facade to prioritize remediation.

📄

Insurance & Warranty Documentation

A third-party infrared report provides objective, time-stamped documentation for insurance claims, warranty disputes, and litigation support.
